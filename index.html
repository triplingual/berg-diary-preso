This is Mary Berg, taken on May 8, 1945 in an unknown location.

Between these two photos, Miriam spent just over two years imprisoned in the Warsaw Ghetto, measuring 1.3 square miles in area and holding somewhere from 400,000 to 450,000 people identified by the Nazis as Jews (or sufficiently Jew-like). Between the raw numbers of people and the residential stock destroyed by the German siege of Warsaw in fall of 1939, the mean number of people per residential room was 7.2. All statistics lie, and this one neglects to tell the story of the thousands of families or individuals living (and dying) on the streets. Berg, however, kept a diary beginning on her 15th birthday and located many of the events she witnessed or heard about. ***move to next slide*** My project is to map and make meaningful Berg's location-specific information.

I add locatable references using geojson.io, with a 1930 map of Warsaw to guide me. I had to make reference also to current maps as well as to other historical maps, including (awkwardly) an unauthorized and poorly done digitization of one of the remarkable maps in Barbara Engelking-Boni and Jacek Leociak's "Warsaw Ghetto: A Guide to the Perished City".

Why I'm Doing This

To Show Both Time and Space in the Ghetto Experience

When this is all done, my hope is that at least some people using the site will have a greater appreciation for the day-to-day grind of the Warsaw Ghetto, for the fact that it wasn't merely a segregating experience but a terrorizing one over a long period of time.

As well, I hope the essay will show Berg's work as both an important resistance effort as well as a documentation of other small, individual resistance efforts.
